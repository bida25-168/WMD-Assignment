Types of debates:

Parliamentary Debate

This is a extemporaneous debating on topics that can change from debate to debate.They are called parliamentary
because they are similar to the debates that occur in the british parliament and are hence used by decision-making bodies.

  • This involves the use of :
    • Points of information(request for clarification question/brief statement to opponent on the floor)
    • Two teams, each with two debaters
    • Six speeches

    Lincoln Douglas Debate

    This is an open type of debate that was influenced by Abraham Lincoln and Stephen A. Douglas in the 1850's. It is
    a one-on-one debate that focuses on arguing for or against an agreed topic while speaking persuasively, clearly and logically.

    It a debate between two people , therefore opinions are voiced out easily and openly. For example, Presidential debates

    Cross-examination Debate

    This is a form of a two-to-two type of debate that is defined as a period between spechees.Here, the opponent answers points of informations
    in the middle of their speech for clarity and undeestanding.This allows them to engage with their opponents
